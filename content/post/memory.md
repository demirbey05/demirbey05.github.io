I saw many of people going into 100 days of "bla bla" program, when they want to learn something. I've been deeply interested with System Programming for a small time. My first meeting with System Programming was 5 month ago. I quitted my data job in February and decided to fill my basic knowledge gaps in computer science. I knew programming but I do not know what socket programming or what thread was. While I was surfing on the net, I came across [Teach Yourself CS](https://teachyourselfcs.com/). Then, I decided to start this program end-to-end.


But program is so extensive, maybe it takes years to fully complete. Then I suddenly saw this paragraph on the site :

> If the idea of self-studying 9 topics over multiple years feels overwhelming, we suggest you focus on just two books: **Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective** and **Designing Data-Intensive Applications**. In our experience, these two books provide incredibly high return on time invested, particularly for self-taught engineers and bootcamp grads working on networked applications. 

I looked these two books and I thought that second book's level is above my knowledge so I decided to stick with **Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective**. I think I made this topic a little longer 😀. Essentially, as I progressed in the book, the system programming felt good. For a while, I decided to pursue my profession as a system programmer or related field such as backend development...etc. So, **I am starting 100 days of System Programming**.🎉🎉🎉

## Tentative Plan

- I am continuing the CSAPP book. It is impossible to finish all book in 100 days, but I am planning to finish at least 3 chapters from the book. The chapters I will finish are 6,7 and 8th chapter.
- As a programming language, I decided to begin with C++ (I am hearing that you said Rust !!!!). I know C++ fundamentals, but I need to enhance my C++ skills. I am planning to finish [A Tour of C++](https://www.amazon.com/Tour-2nd-Depth-Bjarne-Stroustrup/dp/0134997832) and [Data Structures and Algorithms in C++](https://www.amazon.com/Data-Structures-Algorithms-Michael-Goodrich/dp/0470383275/ref=sr_1_9?crid=1DSSKMC1CZ1N5&keywords=data+structures+and+algorithm+analysis+in+c%2B%2B&qid=1695162579&s=books&sprefix=data+str%2Cstripbooks-intl-ship%2C248&sr=1-9) books.
- For practicing, I will use CMU's [15-213](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~213/schedule.html) assignments and [UIUC's CS241](https://courses.engr.illinois.edu/cs241/sp2017/labs.html) labs. 


I am so excited to start such a journey and I am sure that this will has good effects on my further career.
